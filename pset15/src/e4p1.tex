Determine the critical points of the following functions.

\begin{align*}
    f(x, y) = 4 + x^3 + y^3 - 3xy
\end{align*}

\begin{solution}
\begin{align*}
    \nabla f = \begin{bmatrix}
        3x^2 - 3y \\
        3y^2 - 3x
    \end{bmatrix} = \boldsymbol{0}
    3x^2 - 3y &= 0 \\
    3y^2 - 3x &= 0 \\
    y &= x^2 \\
    x^4 - x &= 0 \\
    x &= 0, 1 \\
    y &= 0, 1
\end{align*}

The critical values of $f(x, y)$ is $(0, 0)$ and $(1, 1)$.
\end{solution}
