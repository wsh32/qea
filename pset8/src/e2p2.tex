What happens to $y$ as $t \to \pm\infty$? How does this limiting behavior depend on the sign of $k$?

\begin{solution}\ \\
If $k > 0$:
\begin{align*}
    \lim_{t \to \infty} y &= A \\
    \lim_{t \to -\infty} y &= 0 \\
\end{align*}
If $k < 0$:
\begin{align*}
    \lim_{t \to \infty} y &= 0 \\
    \lim_{t \to -\infty} y &= A \\
\end{align*}
\end{solution}
