Consider the following matrix whose columns lie on the same line: the second column is simply twice the first column.

\begin{align*}
    \boldsymbol{A} = \begin{bmatrix}
        1 & 2 \\
        2 & 4
    \end{bmatrix}
\end{align*}

What is $\det(\boldsymbol{A})$?

\begin{solution}
    \begin{align}
        \det(\boldsymbol{A}) = 0
    \end{align}
\end{solution}
